Athyutthaya

"Ancient Echoes: Ayutthaya"

Discover ancient Siam at the UNESCO-listed Ayutthaya Historical Park. Marvel at majestic temples like Wat Mahathat and explore cultural gems like the Ayutthaya Floating Market. Experience the vibrant Ayutthaya World Heritage Fair for a truly unforgettable journey.

Top Attractions

Wat Mahathat

Wat Mahathat, located in Ayutthaya, Thailand, is an iconic historical site known for its striking ruins and the famous Buddha head entwined in tree roots. Once a grand royal temple, it now offers a glimpse into Thailand's rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore ancient stone chedis (stupas), crumbling brick structures, and sacred relics amidst a peaceful atmosphere. The temple is a key part of the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Ayutthaya Historical Park, making it a must-visit for those interested in Thai history and architecture.

Wat Phra Sri Sanphet

Wat Phra Sri Sanphet, located in Ayutthaya, Thailand, is one of the most significant and majestic temples of the ancient Ayutthaya Kingdom. Known for its three grand chedis (stupas) that dominate the skyline, this former royal temple was once part of the Grand Palace complex and served as a sacred site for royal ceremonies. The temple's impressive ruins, towering stupas, and serene courtyards reflect the architectural grandeur of Ayutthaya's golden age, making it a must-see for history enthusiasts exploring the UNESCO-listed Ayutthaya Historical Park.

Ayutthaya Floating Market

Ayutthaya Floating Market is a vibrant cultural attraction offering a blend of traditional Thai commerce and entertainment. Located in Ayutthaya, it features wooden walkways, canals with boats selling local goods, and stalls offering Thai food, handicrafts, and souvenirs. The market provides a lively, immersive experience of Thailand's floating market culture, set against a scenic, historical backdrop.
