Sukhothai

"Cradle of Civilization: Sukhothai"

Explore the historical richness of Cradle of Civilization. Visit the Sukhothai Historical Park, where the origins of Thai civilization come alive. Discover the ancient temples of Wat Si Chum, Wat Phra Phai Luang, and Wat Mahathat. Explore the nearby Sukhothai Ramkhamhaeng National Museum and take a serene bike ride around the ancient city’s well-preserved ruins and lotus ponds. Consider visiting during the Sukhothai Loy Krathong Festival, where you can witness lantern releases and traditional Thai dances.

Top Attractions

Sukhothai Historical Park

Sukhothai Historical Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the ruins of the ancient Sukhothai Kingdom. Featuring impressive temples, lush gardens, and serene ponds, it offers a glimpse into Thailand’s historical and cultural heritage, making it a must-visit for history enthusiasts.

Sukhothai Ramkhamhaeng National Museum

Sukhothai Ramkhamhaeng National Museum is dedicated to preserving the art and culture of the Sukhothai period. The museum houses a collection of artifacts, including sculptures and pottery, providing valuable insights into the region’s history and the evolution of Thai civilization.

Sukhothai Loy Krathong Festival

Sukhothai Loy Krathong Festival is a vibrant annual celebration held in November, featuring the enchanting sight of lanterns and floating krathongs (decorative floats) released on water. This colorful festival honors the water goddess and showcases traditional performances, making it a memorable cultural experience for visitors.
