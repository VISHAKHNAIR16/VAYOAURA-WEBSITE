Komodo Island

Komodo Island – Home of the Dragons

Komodo Island, part of the Komodo National Park, is famous for being the habitat of the endangered Komodo dragons. The island offers stunning landscapes, with rugged hills, crystal-clear waters, and pristine beaches. Visitors can trek to see the dragons in their natural environment or enjoy activities like snorkeling and diving. Komodo Island is a must-visit for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Top Attractions

Komodo National Park

Komodo National Park, located in Indonesia’s Lesser Sunda Islands, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its incredible biodiversity. It’s home to the famous Komodo dragons, the largest lizards on Earth, and offers breathtaking landscapes, coral reefs, and pristine beaches. The park is a haven for divers, with vibrant marine life and crystal-clear waters. A top destination for nature lovers, adventurers, and wildlife enthusiasts.

Pink Beach

Pink Beach, located on Komodo Island, is famous for its unique pink-hued sand, created by a mix of white sand and crushed red coral. The beach offers crystal-clear waters, making it a great spot for snorkeling and swimming. Surrounded by stunning hills and vibrant marine life, it’s a serene and picturesque destination. Pink Beach is a must-visit for those seeking natural beauty and tranquility.

Rinca Island

Rinca Island, part of Komodo National Park, is known for its population of Komodo dragons. The island offers rugged landscapes, hiking trails, and a chance to observe these magnificent reptiles in their natural habitat. Its clear waters and vibrant coral reefs make it a great spot for snorkeling and diving. Rinca is a less crowded alternative to Komodo Island, offering a peaceful and immersive nature experience.
