Situated in Kalimantan (Borneo), Tanjung Puting National Park is a haven for orangutan conservation. Embark on river cruises to witness these fascinating creatures in their natural habitat.The park is crisscrossed by a network of rivers and waterways, providing a unique opportunity to explore its lush mangroves and diverse ecosystems. In addition to orangutans, visitors may encounter a variety of wildlife, including proboscis monkeys, gibbons, and exotic bird species. Guided tours often include visits to research stations and feeding platforms, where you can observe these incredible primates up close while learning about ongoing conservation efforts to protect their habitat.

Top Attractions

Orangutan Spotting

Take a river cruise through the park to observe orangutans and other wildlife in their natural habitat. As you glide along the meandering rivers, keep your eyes peeled for these magnificent primates swinging gracefully from branch to branch. The thrill of spotting them in the wild, along with the sounds of the rainforest, creates an unforgettable experience that connects you to nature.

Camp Leakey

Visit the famous orangutan research center, where dedicated scientists and conservationists work tirelessly to rehabilitate and protect these endangered creatures. Here, you can learn about the ongoing research efforts and the challenges faced in orangutan conservation. Engaging with the staff and observing the orangutans up close will deepen your understanding of their behavior and the importance of preserving their habitat.

Eco-Tours

Learn about conservation efforts and explore the lush rainforests that are home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. Guided eco-tours provide insights into the delicate ecosystem and the vital role it plays in sustaining wildlife. You'll also have the opportunity to participate in activities that support local conservation initiatives, making your visit not only enjoyable but also impactful.
