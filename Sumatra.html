Sumatra

Sumatra – The Wild Heart

Sumatra is Indonesia’s largest island, home to diverse wildlife, volcanoes, and vast jungles.

Top Attractions

Lake Toba

The world’s largest volcanic lake, Lake Toba is a breathtaking natural wonder formed by a massive eruption thousands of years ago. Surrounded by lush hills and traditional Batak villages, the lake offers a serene escape for visitors seeking tranquility. You can enjoy activities such as swimming, fishing, or simply relaxing by the water while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Bukit Lawang (Orangutans)

Home to endangered Sumatran orangutans, Bukit Lawang is a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts. Trek through the lush rainforest to spot these incredible primates in their natural habitat, and witness their playful interactions in the treetops. The area is also rich in biodiversity, providing opportunities to see other unique species, such as gibbons and exotic birds, making it a true paradise for nature lovers.

Mount Sinabung

An active volcano offering an adventurous trek, Mount Sinabung presents a thrilling challenge for hikers and adventure seekers. As you ascend, you'll be rewarded with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including lush valleys and distant mountains. The experience of standing at the summit, with the rumbling of the volcano beneath your feet, is both exhilarating and humbling, reminding you of the raw power of nature.
